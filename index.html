Document Legalisation
International Documents

Legalisation of
official documents

These services confer legal validity upon South African official public documents, enabling their lawful use outside the Republic for employment, travel and legal purposes.

Apostille by DIRCO
  • Official Document LegalisationLegal validity is conferred upon South African public documents, authorising their use in countries party to the Hague Convention.
  • Accepted Document TypesBirth, marriage and death certificates, academic qualifications, court orders, affidavits and other public documents.
  • Priority Processing AvailableExpedited options are available where there are urgent international deadlines or imminent travel requirements.
Apostille by the High Court
  • Certificate of AuthenticationWhere a destination country is not a signatory to the Hague Convention, a Certificate of Authentication by the High Court is required.
  • Country-Specific GuidanceWe advise on the precise legalisation route required for each destination country to ensure full legal recognition abroad.
  • Comprehensive AssistanceEnd-to-end service management from initial document preparation through to delivery of authenticated documentation.
Drafting of Documents
Document Preparation

Drafting of legal
documents & applications

Professional preparation of legal documents, applications and correspondence — drafted in accordance with applicable South African legislation and managed on your behalf.

# Document / Service Description
i
Domestic Violence Application

Drafting of domestic violence applications in accordance with the Domestic Violence Act 116 of 1998 and the Domestic Violence Amendment Act 14 of 2021.

ii
Harassment Order Application

Preparation of harassment order applications pursuant to the Protection from Harassment Act 17 of 2011, safeguarding your right to live free from harassment.

iii
Small Claims Court Assistance

Legal advice and full assistance with Small Claims Court proceedings. Note: jurisdiction is limited to civil disputes not exceeding R20,000.

iv
Letter of Demand

Preparation of a formal letter of demand in accordance with Section 129 of the National Credit Act (NCA) — an official demand for money lawfully owed.

v
Letter of No Impediment

A legal document certifying current marital status, ordinarily requested by foreign authorities when South African nationals seek to marry abroad. Sourced from the Department of Home Affairs.

vi
UIF Claim Submission

Assistance with Unemployment Insurance Fund (UIF) claim submissions, providing short-term relief to workers who have become unemployed or are unable to work.

vii
SAQA Verification

Facilitation of South African Qualifications Authority (SAQA) verification letters confirming the legitimacy and recognition of qualifications.

+
Facilitate Return of Service

Full facilitation of the return of service for all of the above documents — managed entirely on your behalf.
